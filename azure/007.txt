Anti-Fugitive Ordinance Parade 25-year-old Guangzhou youth to Hong Kong Record: I hope Hong Kong does not fall

The FRODEBU today (9th) launched a demonstration against the Hong Kong Government's amendment of the Fugitive Offenders Ordinance. Police estimated that 15.3 per cent had set off from Victoria Park , while the scholar , Mr Lei Dingming , estimated that the total number of people as at 4.45 pm was about 19.95 . Reporters saw at the scene, in addition to the hong Kong people crying in unison "anti-send" slogan, there is no shortage of Mandarin speakers to join.

Nathan, 25, is a member of the team, he introduced, he is engaged in the machinery industry, is living in Guangzhou, Jiangsu. Coming from Guangzhou this time, Nathan wanted to use his camera to record the Hong Kong people's counter-protester scanti-law parade. "You Hong Kong people are not afraid to do it, " Nathan lamented, though not very well understood Cantonese, but could feel "a collective consciousness".

Nathan, 25, from the mainland, who doesn't understand Cantonese, plans to record the parade with a camera. (Hong Kong 01 reporter photo)

Interviewed by the National Security Agency for retweeting news

He shared his experiences, pointing out that he had been interviewed by national security officials for referring to sensitive news in the Mainland in a micro-letter. So this time to Hong Kong, he specially put on dark glasses, and refused the reporter's request to take a positive photo, fearing that the safety of his family is threatened.

Nathan said that since secondary school , he has been to Hong Kong several times , believing that it retains many of the Lost Chinese traditions of the Mainland , and says , " Cherish Hong Kong , i am afraid of falling here " .
